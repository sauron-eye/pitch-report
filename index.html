England's Supercomputer Fires on All Cylinders Against New Zealand

Rohit's Third Hundred Breaks England's Unbeaten Home Record

The only team capable of ending England's extraordinary home ODI run at Edgbaston turned out to be India, and specifically Rohit Sharma — whose 102 off 109 balls anchored a 337/7 that England's much-vaunted batting line-up could only chase to 306/9. Jasprit Bumrah's opening spell, three for eight in his first four overs, was decisive.

Ben Stokes played an innings of consummate authority — 89 off 79 balls — while Jofra Archer's return of 3/30 against his former countrymen had a narrative charge that the scoreboard could not quite capture. The Rose Bowl crowd rose to Archer all day. He gave them reason to.

England Open the World Cup at Lord's: A Statement at the Home of Cricket

The tournament began where cricket began — at Lord's — and England made certain it began on their terms. Jonny Bairstow's 113-ball 105 set the tempo; Jason Roy's ferocity at the top provided the volume. South Africa were reduced to 207 all out in 39.5 overs and the message was emphatic: this England side are not here to participate.

Bairstow's Brutal Century Sets England Back on Course at Headingley

After back-to-back defeats had threatened to derail England's tournament — a rain-affected draw with Pakistan and a loss to Sri Lanka that still stings — Bairstow's response was to assemble 110 off 100 balls. Sri Lanka's 232/9 was never enough once Jonny arrived at the crease in the second over.

England 21 Jun 2019 Headingley, Leeds
ENG won by 150 runs · Match 24

Morgan's Historic Six-Hitting Spectacle Against Afghanistan

Eoin Morgan struck 17 sixes — a world record in ODI cricket at the time — in a 148 off 71 balls that was as bewildering as it was magnificent. Afghanistan's total of 247/8 was never going to be enough against 397/6; the story of the afternoon belonged entirely to the England captain and his extraordinary, almost incomprehensible assault.

Rohit's Second Hundred of the Tournament Edges Out Australia

India won the contest of the two tournament favourites thanks largely to Rohit Sharma's metronomic 57-ball 57 at the top and then a second-phase acceleration in which he reached his century off 115. Australia's 299 chase was derailed by Shami's 4/35 in the last ten overs — decisive, sudden, and coolly delivered.

IND won by 6 wkts · Match 8

Sharma and Dhawan Open India's Campaign with Controlled Authority

South Africa's 227/9 was a below-par total on a flat Southampton surface, and India's openers reduced the chase to a formality within fifteen overs. Shikhar Dhawan's 67-ball 54 set the tone; Rohit Sharma's unbeaten 122-ball 122 finished it with ten overs to spare. India were never challenged.

India Defeat Pakistan — As They Always Do at World Cups

The rain came. The DLS revised the target. And India won — as India have always won against Pakistan in World Cup cricket, a record stretching back to 1992 and now standing at seven from seven. Rohit Sharma's 140 provided the platform; the rain ensured Pakistan were always chasing what the clouds had made impossible.

India 16 Jun 2019 Old Trafford, Manchester
IND won by 7 wkts · Match 44

Kohli and Rohit's Partnership Clinches India's Semi-Final Spot

Sri Lanka's 264/7 was their best effort of the tournament and it still wasn't enough. Rohit Sharma's tournament tally extended to five hundreds as he reached three figures off 94 balls; Virat Kohli, returning from injury, contributed 82 not out in a partnership of 113 that ended the contest as a competition. India strolled home with 8.1 overs remaining.

Bumrah and Shami Dismantle West Indies in a Rain-Affected Thriller

West Indies had won the toss, watched the rain come twice, and then found themselves facing an Indian bowling attack that showed no mercy under the revised conditions. Jasprit Bumrah's controlled fury — 2/31 off six overs — and Mohammed Shami's lethal final spell of 4/16 ended the Caribbean challenge emphatically on a slow Old Trafford surface.

AUS won by 86 runs · Match 20

Smith's Masterclass Exposes New Zealand's Mid-Tournament Fragility

Steve Smith, playing his first World Cup final, batted as if he owned Lord's. His 89 off 107 balls against a Black Caps attack that had been so formidable all tournament gave Australia a platform of 243/9 that proved, against all expectations, entirely sufficient. Starc's 5/26 did the rest in a Lord's that hummed with discomfort.

Australia 29 Jun 2019 Lord's, London
AUS won by 64 runs · Semi-Final

Smith and Starc Eliminate England — The Host Nation Departs at Edgbaston

England's World Cup campaign ended here — not without fight, not without the crowd's insistence that it could still be different, but conclusively. Smith's 85 and Stoinis's 38-ball 73 powered Australia to 223/9 in 49 overs; Starc then removed Roy, Bairstow and Root in six overs and England's chase of 224, seemingly manageable, became 59 for three. The dream ended at 221 all out.

Australia 11 Jul 2019 Edgbaston, Birmingham
NZ won by 5 wkts · Match 13

Williamson's Quiet Authority Sees Off West Indian Pyrotechnics

West Indies struck 421/7 — the highest total of the tournament at that stage — and still lost. Kane Williamson's 148 off 154 balls was an innings of serene, considered purpose: never frantic, never ornate, always exactly adequate to what the match required. New Zealand reached 424/5 with two balls remaining in what might be the most improbable run-chase of the group stage.

New Zealand 22 Jun 2019 Old Trafford, Manchester
Semi-Finals · ICC World Cup 2015
NZ won by 4 wkts · Semi-Final 1 · Auckland

Grant Elliott's Six: The Moment New Zealand Stopped Losing Semi-Finals

South Africa had never won a World Cup knockout match. In the end it was Grant Elliott — a man born in Zimbabwe, playing for New Zealand, on a last-ball six off Dale Steyn — who made sure they still hadn't. The equation at the start of the final over: 12 needed off 6 balls. What followed over that six-ball sequence — a wide, a dot, a four, a dot, a dot, and then the six — will be replayed by New Zealand supporters for generations. South Africa had scored 281/5; New Zealand 299/6 off 43.1 overs. Steyn, across his nine overs before that last one, had been magnificent. It made the ending more, not less, painful.

New Zealand 24 Mar 2015 Eden Park, Auckland
AUS won by 95 runs · Semi-Final 2 · Sydney

Australia End India's Tournament — and Perhaps an Era — at the SCG

India had won every game at this World Cup. Australia ended that record comprehensively — and perhaps ended something else too: the sense, held since 2011, that this India team were the natural owners of limited-overs cricket. Steve Smith made 65; Glenn Maxwell's 23-ball 23 was the accelerant. Australia's 328/7 proved seventeen runs too many for India's batting depth to overhaul, even with Virat Kohli making 53. Mitchell Johnson's 1/30 off 10 overs told only part of the story; his presence — his sheer physical presence — did the rest. India were 233 all out. The tournament, and the final, belonged to Australia.

Australia 26 Mar 2015 Sydney Cricket Ground
