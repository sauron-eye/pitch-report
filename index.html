ENG won by 104 runs · Match 1 · Tournament Opener

England Open the World Cup at The Oval: Stokes, Archer, and a Statement of Intent

South Africa's Faf du Plessis made the unconventional call to open with leg-spinner Imran Tahir, who dismissed Jonny Bairstow for a golden duck off his second ball. England recovered through half-centuries from Roy (54), Root (51), Morgan (57) and Stokes — who top-scored with 89 off 79 balls and then produced arguably the greatest catch of the tournament to dismiss Phehlukwayo. Jofra Archer took 3/27 as South Africa crumbled to 207 all out; a message was sent to every other side in the competition.

England 30 May 2019 The Oval, London
PAK won by 14 runs · Match 6

Pakistan Shock England at Trent Bridge in the Tournament's First Major Upset

Pakistan posted a formidable 348/8 — Root and Buttler both scored centuries in England's reply of 334/9, making it the highest-scoring World Cup match to that point. But Wahab Riaz's decisive double-strike when England needed 29 off 14 balls sealed a stunning 14-run victory for Pakistan, silencing a sell-out Trent Bridge that had fully expected an England celebration. "Pakistan Zindabad!" echoed long after the stumps were drawn.

Pakistan win 3 Jun 2019 Trent Bridge, Nottingham
ENG won by 8 wkts · Match 19

Root's Hundred Rescues England After Injuries to Roy and Morgan

Jofra Archer and Mark Wood each took three wickets to restrict West Indies to 212/10 in 44.4 overs — the short-ball plan that had looked so menacing earlier in the tournament found no purchase here. Jason Roy tweaked his hamstring and Morgan suffered a back spasm, forcing a batting reshuffle. Into the breach stepped Joe Root, who opened and played a crunching unbeaten 100 to lead England home with 101 balls to spare. A controlled, professional performance at the right moment.

England 14 Jun 2019 The Rose Bowl, Southampton
ENG won by 150 runs · Match 24

Morgan's Historic Six-Hitting Spectacle Against Afghanistan

Eoin Morgan struck 17 sixes — a world record in ODI cricket — in a 148 off 71 balls that was as bewildering as it was magnificent. England posted 397/6; Afghanistan's 247/8 was never going to be sufficient. The story of the afternoon belonged entirely to the England captain: 17 maximums over the boundary, each one a more emphatic declaration than the last that this side intended to win this tournament in their own audacious terms.

Record 18 Jun 2019 Old Trafford, Manchester
IND won by 31 runs · Match 38

Roy Returns But India End England's Unbeaten Home ODI Record at Edgbaston

The return of a fit Jason Roy — 66 off 57 balls — and a Bairstow century (111) gave England 337/7, their best total of the tournament. But Rohit Sharma (102) and Virat Kohli (66) made India's reply of 306/5 look stately rather than a defeat, and Liam Plunkett's 3/55 could not prevent the first blemish on England's home ODI record in three years. India remained the only side capable of treating England's vaunted attack with something approaching contempt.

India win 30 Jun 2019 Edgbaston, Birmingham
ENG won by 119 runs · Match 41

Twenty-Seven Years of Waiting End at Chester-le-Street: England Are in the Semi-Finals

By the time Mark Wood's final over had removed New Zealand's last two batsmen at Riverside Ground on a sunny Durham afternoon, England had won by 119 runs and qualified for their first World Cup semi-final since 1992. Twenty-seven years. One afternoon in Chester-le-Street. Jonny Bairstow, for the second consecutive match in four days, had scored a century.

England 3 Jul 2019 Emirates Riverside, Chester-le-Street
ENG won by 8 wkts · Match 27

Sri Lanka Stun the Hosts at Headingley by Twenty Runs

They were an eliminated side, effectively, or close enough to one that the distinction barely registered. Sri Lanka arrived at Headingley with one victory from five matches, a bowling attack of modest pace, and a batting order that had, across five outings, failed to produce a performance its captain could describe without qualification. And then Lasith Malinga — thirty-five years old, bowling with a slingy, round-arm action that physics insists should not produce the results it produces — removed England's top three with four wickets across ten overs, and Dhananjaya de Silva swept up the middle, and Sri Lanka won by twenty runs. The tournament, which had appeared to have arranged itself into a predictable shape, disagreed.

England 21 Jun 2019 Headingley, Leeds
India Matches
IND won by 6 wkts · Match 8

Rohit and Chahal Set the Tone as India Open with a Comfortable Win

South Africa's 227/9 was a below-par total on a flat Southampton surface and India were never troubled. Rohit Sharma made an unbeaten 122 off 144 balls, finishing the game with ten overs to spare, while leg-spinner Yuzvendra Chahal's 4/51 had been the decisive intervention with the ball. A quiet, controlled start to India's campaign that told you more about their depth than their ambition.

India 5 Jun 2019 The Rose Bowl, Southampton
IND won by 36 runs · Match 14

Dhawan's Century and Kohli's Brilliance Destroy Australia's Unbeaten Record

Shikhar Dhawan's 117 off 109 balls was the centrepiece of India's 352/5 — he and Rohit Sharma (57) put on 127 for the first wicket before Virat Kohli's 82 off 77 and Hardik Pandya's explosive 48 off 27 took India beyond 350. Australia's reply of 316 all out was competitive on paper but never on the day; Bumrah's 3/61 and Kuldeep Yadav's control strangled the middle overs. India's second win emphatically announced their intentions.

India 9 Jun 2019 The Oval, London
IND won by 89 runs (D/L) · Match 22

India Defeat Pakistan — As They Always Do at World Cups

India posted 336/5, anchored by Rohit Sharma's 140 — his third hundred of the tournament — before rain twice interrupted Pakistan's chase and Duckworth-Lewis revised the target to one beyond reach. India won by 89 runs under D/L: their seventh World Cup win from seven against Pakistan, a record stretching unbroken since 1992. No other rivalry in world sport carries quite this weight; no other scoreline quite so reliably produces the same outcome.

India 16 Jun 2019 Old Trafford, Manchester
IND won by 125 runs · Match 34

Bumrah and Shami Dismantle West Indies as India Close In on the Semis

Virat Kohli won the toss and batted first on a surface favouring the bowlers; KL Rahul (48) and Kohli himself provided the platform before India posted 268/7. Jasprit Bumrah's controlled opening burst and Mohammed Shami's lethal death overs reduced West Indies from 147/4 to 143 all out in 34.2 overs. A 125-run win, India's most emphatic yet — and a virtual semi-final booking.

India 27 Jun 2019 Old Trafford, Manchester
IND won by 7 wkts · Match 44

Rohit's Fifth Hundred Clinches India's Semi-Final Spot at Headingley

Rohit Sharma became the first batsman in history to score five centuries in a single World Cup, reaching three figures off 94 balls against Sri Lanka's total of 264/7. KL Rahul made a serene 111* alongside him as India chased down the target in 43.3 overs with seven wickets in hand. Angelo Mathews had made his third ODI century against India in the Sri Lanka innings — all to no avail. India, fully through, had set themselves apart as the tournament's dominant batting side.

India 6 Jul 2019 Headingley, Leeds
Australia & New Zealand
AUS won by 86 runs · Match 37 · Lord's

Carey Steers Australia Through the Boult Hat-Trick to Seal Semi-Final Place

Australia were reduced to 92/5 before Alex Carey — player of the match with 71 off 72 — and Usman Khawaja rebuilt to 243/9 on a turning Lord's pitch. Trent Boult took a stunning hat-trick in the Australian innings that threatened to derail the chase entirely, but New Zealand's reply crumbled to 157 all out in 43.4 overs. Mitchell Starc's unrelenting pressure throughout confirmed Australia as the tournament's most dangerous side with the ball.

Australia 29 Jun 2019 Lord's, London
ENG won by 8 wkts · Semi-Final 2

Roy and Bairstow Demolish Australia to Send England to Their First Final Since 1992

England's bowlers — Woakes 3/20, Rashid 3/54 — dismissed Australia for 223, with only Steve Smith offering resistance with 85 before being run out by a sharp Jos Buttler direct hit. Jason Roy's 85 off 65 and Bairstow's 34 off 38 set up the chase with merciless intent; England reached 226/2 in 32.1 overs with Jonny Bairstow and Joe Root finishing the job. A clinical dismantling that sent Edgbaston into rapture and Australia home early.

England 11 Jul 2019 Edgbaston, Birmingham
NZ won by 5 runs · Match 29

Brathwaite's Extraordinary Hundred Comes Up Five Runs Short of the Impossible

Kane Williamson's 148 off 154 balls — his tournament-defining innings — rescued New Zealand from 7/2 to post 291/8. West Indies then stood at 164/7, the game all but over, before Carlos Brathwaite's stunning first ODI century (101 off 82) brought them to within six runs off the final ball. Trent Boult held a brilliant catch just inside the boundary off the penultimate delivery to end it; New Zealand won by five runs in one of the greatest group-stage matches in World Cup history. Brathwaite stood, inconsolable, at the crease.

New Zealand 22 Jun 2019 Old Trafford, Manchester
Semi-Finals · ICC World Cup 2015
NZ won by 4 wkts · Semi-Final 1 · Auckland

Grant Elliott's Six: The Moment New Zealand Stopped Losing Semi-Finals

South Africa had never won a World Cup knockout match. In the end it was Grant Elliott — a man born in Zimbabwe, playing for New Zealand — who made sure they still hadn't. The equation at the start of the final over: 12 needed off 6 balls, Dale Steyn bowling. What followed was a wide, a dot, a four, a dot, a dot, and then a six over long-on that will be replayed by New Zealand supporters forever. South Africa had scored 281/5; New Zealand reached 299/6 off 43.1 overs. Steyn, across his nine overs before that last, had been magnificent. It made the ending more, not less, painful.

New Zealand 24 Mar 2015 Eden Park, Auckland
AUS won by 95 runs · Semi-Final 2 · Sydney

Australia End India's Tournament — and Perhaps an Era — at the SCG

India had won every game at this World Cup. Australia ended that record — and perhaps ended something else. Steve Smith made 65; Glenn Maxwell's 23-ball 23 was the accelerant that pushed Australia to 328/7. India's reply of 233 all out, despite a Virat Kohli 53, was dismantled by Mitchell Johnson's sheer intimidating presence and Pat Cummins's 2/60. It was Mitchell Starc's 5/27 — including the dismissal of Rohit Sharma first ball — that broke India's back early. Australia were heading to the MCG for a final that, by that point, felt inevitable.

Australia 26 Mar 2015 Sydney Cricket Ground
